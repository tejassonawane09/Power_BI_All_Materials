
# KPIs & Business Metrics


## 1. Total Sales

### Real Business Example
A company sells 500 laptops at ₹60,000 each.

Total Sales = 500 × 60,000 = ₹3,00,00,000

This represents the total revenue collected from customers.

### What It Tells Management
- Business size
- Revenue growth trend
- Market demand strength

### Interview Answer
Total Sales represents the total revenue generated from customers. It helps measure overall business growth and performance trends.

---

## 2. Total Cost

### Real Business Example
To generate ₹3 crore sales, the company spent:
- ₹2.5 crore on product purchase
- ₹20 lakh on salaries
- ₹10 lakh on rent
- ₹5 lakh on logistics

Total Cost = ₹2.85 crore

### What It Tells Management
- Operational efficiency
- Expense control
- Sustainability of profit

### Interview Answer
Total Cost includes all expenses incurred to generate revenue and helps evaluate operational efficiency.

---

## 3. Total Profit

### Real Business Example
Sales = ₹3 crore  
Cost = ₹2.85 crore  

Profit = ₹15 lakh

### What It Tells Management
- Financial health
- Business sustainability
- Strategy effectiveness

### Interview Answer
Total Profit is calculated as Total Sales minus Total Cost and reflects the company’s financial success.

---

## 4. Quantity

### Real Business Example
Company sold 500 units.

### Why It Matters
- Measures demand
- Identifies product popularity
- Tracks volume trends

### Interview Answer
Quantity represents the number of units sold and helps analyze demand patterns.

---

## 5. Average Metrics

### Real Business Example
Total Sales = ₹3 crore  
Orders = 500  

Average Order Value = ₹60,000

### Why It Matters
- Understands customer behavior
- Identifies spending trends

### Interview Answer
Average metrics provide insights into typical customer behavior and transaction value.

---

## 6. Profit Margin %

### Real Business Example
Profit = ₹15 lakh  
Sales = ₹3 crore  

Margin = 5%

Meaning: For every ₹100 sale, ₹5 is profit.

### Why It Matters
- Measures efficiency
- Evaluates pricing strategy
- Assesses cost control

### Interview Answer
Profit Margin percentage measures how efficiently revenue is converted into profit.

---

## 7. Target vs Actual

### Real Business Example
Target Sales = ₹3.5 crore  
Actual Sales = ₹3 crore  

Missed target by ₹50 lakh.

### Why It Matters
- Evaluates goal achievement
- Identifies performance gaps

### Interview Answer
Target vs Actual compares planned performance with actual results to measure business success.

---

## 8. Thresholds

### Real Business Example
Profit Margin thresholds:
- Above 15% → Good
- 10–15% → Moderate
- Below 10% → Risk

### Why It Matters
- Enables quick decision-making
- Highlights performance risks

### Interview Answer
Thresholds define performance ranges that help quickly identify strong or weak performance.

---

## 9. KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)

### What They Are
KPIs are the most important measurable values that reflect business performance.

### Common KPIs
- Total Sales
- Total Profit
- Profit Margin
- Growth Rate

### Interview Answer
KPIs are measurable indicators aligned with business objectives that help leadership monitor performance and make strategic decisions.

---

## Final Business Summary

Sales shows revenue scale.  
Cost shows expenses.  
Profit shows financial success.  
Margin shows efficiency.  
Targets show expectations.  
Thresholds show risk levels.  
KPIs combine all of them into decision-focused metrics.
