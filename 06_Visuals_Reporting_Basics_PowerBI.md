# Visuals & Reporting Basics 

# 1. Card Visual

## What It Is

A Card visual displays a single important numeric value in a large,
clear format.

## When To Use

-   To show key KPIs such as Total Sales, Total Profit, or Profit
    Margin.
-   When management needs a quick performance snapshot.

## Why It Matters

Cards provide immediate visibility of business performance. They are
usually placed at the top of dashboards to highlight critical metrics.

## Business Example

A dashboard showing: - Total Sales: ₹3 Crore - Total Profit: ₹15 Lakh -
Profit Margin: 5%

These help management quickly understand overall performance.

## Interview Answer

A Card visual is used to display a single key performance indicator
clearly and prominently for quick decision-making.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Table Visual

## What It Is

A Table displays detailed row-level information similar to Excel.

## When To Use

-   When detailed data validation is required.
-   When users need to see transaction-level data.

## Why It Matters

Tables allow managers to verify numbers and review detailed breakdowns
behind summary visuals.

## Business Example

Product Name \| Sales \| Cost \| Profit

This allows detailed inspection of each product's performance.

## Interview Answer

A Table visual is used for displaying detailed structured data for
validation and granular analysis.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Matrix Visual

## What It Is

A Matrix is an advanced table that supports hierarchical grouping and
drill-down.

## When To Use

-   When multi-level grouping is required.
-   For financial or summarized reports.

## Why It Matters

It supports structured analysis such as Year → Quarter → Month
breakdown.

## Business Example

Region → Product → Sales Year → Quarter → Month → Revenue

## Interview Answer

A Matrix visual is used for hierarchical and cross-tab analysis where
grouped data insights are required.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Bar Chart

## What It Is

A Bar Chart displays data using horizontal bars.

## When To Use

-   Comparing categories.
-   When category names are long.
-   Ranking analysis.

## Why It Matters

It clearly shows which category performs best or worst.

## Business Example

Profit by Product Category displayed horizontally for ranking.

## Interview Answer

A Bar Chart is used to compare performance across categories in a clear
and visually structured way.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Column Chart

## What It Is

A Column Chart displays data using vertical bars.

## When To Use

-   Comparing categories.
-   Showing yearly or periodic comparisons.

## Why It Matters

It provides a clear comparison of values across discrete categories.

## Business Example

Sales by Year comparison using vertical bars.

## Interview Answer

A Column Chart is used for category comparison and periodic performance
visualization.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Line Chart

## What It Is

A Line Chart displays data trends over time.

## When To Use

-   Tracking monthly or yearly trends.
-   Identifying growth or decline patterns.

## Why It Matters

Business decisions are often trend-based rather than single-value based.

## Business Example

Monthly Sales trend over 3 years.

## Interview Answer

A Line Chart is used to analyze trends and monitor performance changes
over time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Slicer

## What It Is

A Slicer is an interactive filtering visual.

## When To Use

-   To allow users to filter by Year, Region, Product, etc.

## Why It Matters

It makes dashboards interactive and user-driven.

## Business Example

User selects Region = North, and all visuals update automatically.

## Interview Answer

A Slicer enables interactive filtering, allowing users to dynamically
explore report data.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 8. Filters Pane

## What It Is

The Filters Pane applies filters at visual, page, or report level.

## Why It Matters

It controls data visibility and ensures focused analysis.

## Levels

-   Visual-level filter
-   Page-level filter
-   Report-level filter

## Interview Answer

The Filters Pane allows controlled data filtering at multiple levels to
support focused reporting.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 9. Tooltips

## What It Is

Tooltips display additional information when hovering over visuals.

## Why It Matters

They provide extra context without cluttering the report.

## Business Example

Hover over product bar → Shows Profit, Margin, Growth %

## Interview Answer

Tooltips provide contextual insights without overcrowding the dashboard
layout.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 10. Sorting

## What It Is

Sorting arranges data in ascending or descending order.

## Why It Matters

Correct sorting ensures accurate ranking and meaningful visual
interpretation.

## Business Example

Top 5 products sorted by highest sales.

## Interview Answer

Sorting ensures visuals present data in a structured and meaningful
ranking order.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Professional Summary

Cards → Highlight key KPIs\
Tables → Show detailed data\
Matrix → Hierarchical analysis\
Bar/Column → Category comparison\
Line → Trend analysis\
Slicer → Interactive filtering\
Filters Pane → Controlled filtering\
Tooltips → Extra context\
Sorting → Proper ranking
